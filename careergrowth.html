Converting Internship Into a Full-Time Offer and Negotiating Salary:

Hi COZYGIRLZ. My name’s Sarah and I am about to graduate college with a degree in Marketing. I have been interning at a company for about six months and they offered me a job once I graduate. This is an amazing opportunity, however, the starting salary is lower than I expected and is less than an offer I got from another company. Do you guys have any tips on how to negotiate for a higher salary? I love the company I am currently at, but if they cannot match the other company I am going to have to go with the other job.

Reflection on Networking:

I’m always the youngest person in the room and it feels really difficult to break into conversations. The inner voice in my head is always saying that I’m ‘too young’ and ‘won’t be taken seriously.’ How can I use my youthfulness as a strength and not a weakness to network and make meaningful connections that can help the trajectory of my career as I get older?

Landing My First Job Before Graduation:

Here is how I landed my first job in market research one year before graduation, I never understood the importance of speaking up about my work and branding myself. If you work hard and put a lot of effort into your work, the work should speak for itself, right? Well, here is the truth: We live in a society where “closed mouths don’t get fed.” I once had a hard time raising my voice and amplifying the significant accomplishments and contributions I have made in the industry. Over time I realized that if I didn’t tackle this insecurity that a number of opportunities that have come my way in my career thus far would have never been a possibility. Moral of the story is to invest time in building your profile on LinkedIn, presenting your projects and accomplishments in a compelling way. Nowadays, you can get a pre-built website on Wix.com or other vendors to showcase any projects you want, even stories about your lifestyle. It definitely makes a difference!
